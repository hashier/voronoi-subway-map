Subway station quirks

  • Stalingrad — Paris still has 2 metro stations named for the battle
  • 栄 — Nagoya, Japan. A single kanji character meaning "prosperity"
  • Sé — São Paulo. Shortest Latin-script station name (2 characters)
  • Lehel — Munich. The only palindrome station name found
  • 424 stations worldwide have no name at all

Data cleanup

OpenStreetMap mappers often tag individual platforms, entrances, or line intersections as separate station nodes. We merge same-name stations within 500m into a single point. Here's what that removed:

DatasetBeforeAfterRemoved
Subways (strict)17,97716,1951,782 (9.9%)
Subways20,42618,0582,368 (11.6%)
All rail114,87084,87729,993 (26.1%)
Airports46,06445,861203 (0.4%)

Tram and light rail stops had the most duplicates (26%). Airports were the cleanest. The worst offenders by city:

CityExtra nodesTypical cause
Marseille97Nearly every station had 5 nodes (one per entrance)
Singapore68Multiple platforms tagged as separate stations
Paris58Line intersections mapped as separate nodes

Data from OpenStreetMap via Overpass API. Regional breakdowns use approximate bounding boxes, so per-continent numbers are estimates (border regions like Turkey or Central America may be miscategorized). Station and airport totals are exact — or at least as exact as OpenStreetMap gets.
